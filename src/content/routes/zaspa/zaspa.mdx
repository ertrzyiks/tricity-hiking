## Starting point

The route begins at the end of _Gdańsk Zaspa_ SKM train station. The easiest
way to get there is to take an SKM train. Alternatively, you can take
a bus 127, 227 or 158 to _Zaspa SKM_ stop or a tram to _Wojska Polskiego_ stop.
The tram stop is on the other side of the rails so if you choose this option you
need to go up and cross the viaduct first.

## Introduction to Zaspa

The Zaspa district is famous for its murals. The monumental paintings on the walls of the blocks
of flats attract visitors from all over the world. You can book a guided tour to see the murals and hear
the stories behind them. You can also explore the area on your own looking at the articles from
https://muralegdanskzaspa.pl/ while walking around.

The murals refer to the rich local history. Two major themes can be observed throughout the collection.
The first one is the history of the district itself. The area, once outskirts of Gdańsk, was the perfect
place for the airport. The aviation theme is present in the murals but also the street names.
The second theme is the role of Solidarity movement in the history of Gdańsk and Poland.


## First Murals

The first mural you will see is the one with the Airship, located on the wall of the block number 12.
